# Market Analysis

## The Problem

Hiring is broken at the screening stage. Resumes filter for credentials, not capability. The result:

- **Bad hires cost 30% of first-year salary** (US Dept of Labor)
- **Average cost-per-hire is $4,700** (SHRM)
- **Average time-to-hire is 44 days** (LinkedIn)
- Soft skills — the #1 predictor of job success per most studies — are invisible on paper
- Hiring managers spend an average of 7 seconds on a resume

### Addressable Market

**Total Addressable Market (TAM):** All hiring globally = $750B

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):** Tech hiring in US + India where AI screening adds value
- ~5M tech hires/year
- Average screening cost: ~$500/hire
- SAM = ~$2.5B

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM):** What we can realistically capture in 3 years
- 0.1-0.5% of SAM = $2.5M-$12.5M

## Why Now

### Tailwinds

1. **AI-native hiring is emerging** — Mercor raised $32M (2024) for AI-driven talent matching, validating the market
2. **Remote hiring is normalized** — post-COVID, companies hire globally and need scalable screening
3. **Regulatory push** — NYC LL144, EU AI Act, Illinois AIPA all require bias audits for AI hiring tools. Companies need compliant solutions. Our built-in ABLEIST framework is a regulatory feature.
4. **LLM costs are plummeting** — GPT-4o-mini is 100x cheaper than GPT-4 was 18 months ago. Long AI conversations are now economically viable.
5. **Resume fatigue** — both candidates and hiring managers are frustrated with the resume-driven process

### Headwinds

1. **Trust deficit** — people are skeptical of AI judging them (mitigation: transparency, candidate sees their own profile)
2. **Regulatory uncertainty** — laws are still forming; could become more restrictive (mitigation: bias detection built-in from day one)
3. **Incumbent inertia** — companies have existing ATS/recruitment workflows (mitigation: integrate, don't replace)

## Target Customer

### Primary: Mid-size tech companies (50-500 employees)

- Hire frequently enough to need tooling
- Small enough that HR team is overwhelmed
- Tech-forward enough to adopt AI tools
- Budget: $300-2,000/month is a non-event vs. current recruitment spend

### Secondary: Recruitment agencies

- Process high volumes of candidates
- Need efficient screening at scale
- Already paying for screening tools
- Budget: higher, enterprise pricing

### Initial Beachhead: Indian tech startups hiring for entry-level roles

- Massive volume (hundreds of applicants per role)
- Resume quality is inconsistent
- Soft skills matter enormously for client-facing roles
- Price-sensitive market validates low-cost approach
- Caste/gender bias is a documented problem — our ABLEIST integration is a differentiator

## Market Validation Signals

1. **Mercor ($32M Series A)** — AI talent platform. Proves VCs believe in AI-driven hiring.
2. **HireVue ($100M+ revenue)** — Video interviewing + AI. Proves enterprises will pay for AI assessment.
3. **LinkedIn Recruiter ($10K+/year)** — Companies pay significant money for better candidate signal.
4. **Indeed/Glassdoor ($3B+ revenue)** — Massive spend on finding candidates. Screening is the bottleneck.
5. **ABLEIST paper findings** — 99.7% of LLM hiring conversations contain bias. Current safety tools catch 0%. There's a gap in the market for bias-aware hiring AI.
