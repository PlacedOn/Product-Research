# Go-to-Market Strategy

## The Cold Start Problem

PlacedOn is a two-sided marketplace. You need candidates AND employers. Neither side shows up without the other.

**Solution: Don't launch as a marketplace. Launch as a B2B tool.**

## Phase 1: Beachhead (Months 1-6)

### Target: 5-10 Indian tech startups hiring entry-level developers

**Why this segment:**
- High volume (100+ applicants per role)
- Resume quality is wildly inconsistent
- Soft skills matter for client-facing roles
- Cost-sensitive (appreciates cheap solution)
- Caste/gender bias is a real documented problem — ABLEIST integration is a genuine selling point
- You likely have network access to founders in this space

**Strategy:**
1. Identify 10 companies actively hiring
2. Offer **free pilot**: "We'll screen your next 50 applicants for free. You compare our profiles against your own interview results."
3. Run the AI interviews, generate atomic trait profiles
4. Compare: Did PlacedOn profiles predict who performed well in the actual human interviews?
5. If correlation is strong → convert to paid customer
6. If correlation is weak → iterate on the system before scaling

**What you need for this phase:**
- Working MVP (AI interviewer + profile generation)
- 5-10 warm intros to hiring managers
- Willingness to do things that don't scale (manual profile review, hands-on onboarding)

**Success criteria:**
- 3+ companies want to continue after free pilot
- >70% correlation between AI profiles and human interview outcomes
- Candidates complete >80% of interviews (they don't drop out mid-way)
- Internal consistency: same candidate interviewed twice produces similar profiles
- Self-validation: >80% of candidates agree their profile "feels accurate"

**Parallel track: College partnerships**
- Identify tier 2-3 engineering colleges (not IITs)
- Offer: "Free verified profile for your graduates. They can share with any employer."
- Position as: career service, not competitor to placement cells
- Goal: build candidate supply independent of employer demand
- 50-100 college-sourced profiles by end of Phase 1 gives you ammunition for Phase 2

## Phase 2: Paid Customers (Months 7-12)

### Convert pilots to paying customers

**Pricing for this phase:**
- $299/month for 50 profiles — low enough that it's an easy yes
- Offer annual discount: $249/month paid annually

**Expansion within accounts:**
- Once one team uses it, introduce to other hiring managers at the same company
- Offer Growth tier ($799) when they hit volume limits

**New customer acquisition:**
- Case studies from pilot phase ("Company X reduced time-to-hire by 40%")
- Founder-led sales (you personally demo to each prospect)
- LinkedIn content: share insights about soft skills in hiring, subtle AI bias findings
- Target: tech startup communities (YC alumni, Indian startup Slack groups, Twitter/X)

**Success criteria:**
- 15-25 paying customers
- <5% monthly churn
- At least 2 Growth-tier customers

## Phase 3: Marketplace Transition (Months 12-24)

### Add the candidate side

Once you have enough employers, flip the script for candidates:

**Candidate value prop:** "Interview once with PlacedOn. Your profile goes to 50+ companies actively hiring. No more tailoring resumes."

**Acquisition channels:**
- College placement cells (partner with 5-10 engineering colleges in India)
- Job seeker communities (Reddit, LinkedIn, Discord servers)
- Referral program ("Get interviewed → refer a friend → skip the queue for employer matching")

**Monetization shift:**
- Employers still pay (subscription or per-hire fee)
- Candidates use for free (they're the product's supply side)
- Premium candidate features later (profile analytics, interview coaching based on AI feedback)

## Phase 4: Scale (Months 24+)

### Expand along two axes

**Geographic:** India → Southeast Asia → US/EU
**Vertical:** Tech → operations → customer service → sales

**Enterprise sales:**
- Dedicated sales hire
- SOC 2 compliance (required for enterprise)
- ATS integrations (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday)
- Custom trait frameworks per company

## Messaging Framework

### For Employers

**Headline:** "See who your candidates actually are — not just what's on their resume"

**Supporting points:**
- AI conducts a 40-minute adaptive interview that reveals thinking patterns, communication style, and soft skills
- Profiles are specific and actionable ("strong system thinker who struggles with ambiguity" not "4/5 technical skills")
- Built-in bias protection — independently validated against 8 ABLEIST harm metrics
- 10x cheaper than traditional screening

### For Candidates

**Headline:** "Interview once. Get seen by hundreds of companies."

**Supporting points:**
- One 40-minute conversation replaces writing dozens of cover letters
- Show employers who you actually are, not what fits in a PDF
- Fair assessment — the AI is tested for bias across disability, gender, nationality, and caste
- You see your own profile and can add context

## Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Companies don't trust AI profiles | Free pilots with direct comparison to human interviews |
| Candidates won't do a 40-min interview | Frame as "one interview for all companies" — saves time overall |
| Regulatory pushback | ABLEIST framework + transparency (candidates see their own profiles) |
| Mercor or similar enters this exact space | Speed + technical moat (Markovian approach) + bias framework |
| Can't prove outcome correlation | Partner closely with pilot companies to track hires over 6+ months |
