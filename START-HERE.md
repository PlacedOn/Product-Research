## What Changed From Your Initial Idea

### You said: "It will be voice-based"
**Research finding:** Build text first, add voice in Phase 2.
- Reason: Voice adds complexity (latency, accents, TTS cost) that shouldn't block core validation
- Cost impact: Voice costs $0.33–0.48/interview vs. $0.03 LLM-only (but still 100x cheaper than human)
- Timeline: Text MVP in 4-6 weeks. Voice in Phase 2 (+3-4 weeks)

### You said: "LinkedIn-style for employers"
**Research finding:** Keep it minimal. No clutter.
- Only 3 screens: job feed, saved pile, profile page
- No social features, no posts feed
- Achievements are on the profile, not in a separate feed

### You said: "How do we get candidates?"
**Research finding:** College partnerships parallel to employer pilots.
- Partner with tier 2-3 colleges (not IITs) — 3,500+ colleges in India
- Position as: "gives your students a verified profile they can share anywhere"
- Recruit candidates independent of employer demand

### You said: "Is this viable?"
**Research finding:** Yes. Strongly viable if execution is good.
- Year 1: -$25K (need seed runway)
- Year 2: $420K profit
- Year 3: $3.6M profit
- Gross margins: 87–98%
- Can be bootstrapped if you hit 11 paying customers within 10 months

## The Honest Reality Check

What can go wrong (read `problems/critical-risks.md` for full details):

1. **Markov contraction loses information** — one bad state update compounds through the whole interview
2. **Voice latency >2 seconds** — conversation breaks down
3. **Indian accent STT fails** — transcripts are garbage
4. **Profiles don't predict performance** — entire value prop collapses
5. **Cold start problem** — no candidates without employers, no employers without candidates
6. **Campus placement cells block you** — they control fresher hiring in India
7. **Candidates game the interview** — Reddit will share "how to pass PlacedOn"

None of these are unsolvable. They're just real. Plan for them.
