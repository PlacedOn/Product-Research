Use this as the Figma prompt/direction for the landing page.

```text
Design a public landing page for PlacedOn at route "/".

Context:
- This is not the candidate dashboard.
- The product structure is:
  - "/" = public landing page
  - "/candidate" = candidate dashboard
  - "/employer" = hirer dashboard
- The landing page goal is one primary conversion: Start an Interview
- Audience priority is candidate-first, especially fresh grads and early-career candidates
- Secondary audience is employers, but they must not dominate the page

Core product message:
PlacedOn helps candidates get hired through an AI interview that generates a verified skill profile. Companies discover them based on demonstrated ability, not resume claims.

What the page must communicate in the first 5-10 seconds:
1. This replaces resumes with an AI interview
2. The interview turns into a verified profile
3. Companies can find candidates based on real ability
4. The main action is to start an interview now

Tone:
- Trustworthy
- Calm
- Modern
- High-signal
- Human, not robotic
- Optimistic, not flashy
- Avoid generic “AI startup” aesthetics

Visual direction:
- Candidate-first and conversion-focused
- Cleaner and more trustworthy than a glassmorphism dashboard
- Use a minimal premium SaaS style with warm confidence
- No neon, no cyberpunk, no overly futuristic UI
- No cluttered bento overload on the homepage
- Use strong whitespace and clear reading flow
- Use rounded cards and soft surfaces
- Use subtle motion cues only if implied, not busy animations

Color palette:
- Deep green: #0A6847
- Bright green: #16A34A
- Mint: #DCFCE7
- Pale green background: #F0FDF4
- White: #FFFFFF
- Near-black text: #1A1A1A
- Warm gray: #F5F5F5
- Secondary text slate: #64748B
- Optional soft gold accent: #FACC15
- Avoid purple-heavy palettes
- Avoid heavy gradients
- Keep contrast strong and readable

Typography:
- Use Plus Jakarta Sans or Manrope
- Headings bold and crisp
- Body text highly readable
- The design should feel premium, not playful
- Clear hierarchy is critical

Create desktop and mobile versions.

Page structure from top to bottom:

1. Navbar
- PlacedOn logo
- Nav links: How It Works, For Candidates, For Companies, Trust, Pricing
- Right-side actions:
  - Log In (secondary)
  - Start Interview (primary)
- Sticky header with white background and subtle bottom border

2. Hero section
- Primary headline should clearly state the core value
- Recommended headline direction:
  "Your skills should get you hired. Not your resume."
- Subheadline should explain:
  AI interviews you, builds a verified profile, and helps companies find you based on demonstrated ability
- Primary CTA: Start Interview
- Secondary CTA: I’m Hiring
- Include one trust-supporting micro-line near the CTA:
  "Role-based interview. No trick questions. Takes about 30 minutes."
- Hero visual should show:
  - AI interview conversation
  - skill signals being extracted
  - profile card being generated
- The visual should feel product-like and explanatory, not decorative
- No stock photos
- Use illustration or clean UI storytelling
- Make the CTA area feel dominant and obvious

3. Social proof / credibility strip
- Immediately below hero
- Show trust elements such as:
  - Verified skill profile
  - Evidence-backed assessment
  - Bias-checked system
  - Candidate-controlled visibility
- Can also include placeholder company logos if needed, but they should not feel fake or overclaimed

4. How it works
- 3-step candidate flow
- Step 1: Choose your role and start the interview
- Step 2: AI turns your answers into a verified profile
- Step 3: Companies discover you based on fit
- Each step needs:
  - short title
  - one sentence explanation
  - simple visual/icon
- This section should be extremely easy to scan

5. Sample profile / proof section
- This is one of the most important sections
- Show what the candidate actually gets after the interview
- Include a polished sample “verified profile” card with:
  - professional summary
  - top strengths
  - confidence indicators
  - evidence-backed skills
  - verified achievements tag
- The purpose is to make the output tangible
- A user should think: “Oh, this is what I’m getting if I start.”

6. Why candidates should trust this
- Address skepticism directly
- 3 or 4 trust blocks:
  - Evidence, not guesswork
  - Bias-checked assessment
  - You control what employers see
  - Retake as you grow
- This section should feel calm and transparent
- Avoid corporate compliance visuals; keep it product-trust oriented

7. For candidates
- Focus on candidate value, not features for feature’s sake
- Include 4 to 6 cards:
  - AI-inferred skills
  - professional summary generated for you
  - growth over time
  - visibility controls
  - application and offer tracking
  - verified achievements
- Keep copy short and benefit-led

8. For companies
- Shorter than candidate section
- Enough to reassure hirers that the platform is serious
- Include 3 to 4 blocks:
  - hire based on evidence
  - role-specific fit matching
  - better than keyword screening
  - candidate comparison with proof
- Include a secondary CTA: I’m Hiring

9. Final CTA section
- Strong close
- Dark green background with white text
- Headline direction:
  "Start with one interview. Let your skills do the talking."
- Primary CTA: Start Interview
- Secondary CTA: Learn How It Works or I’m Hiring

10. Footer
- Simple, clean, product-company footer
- Links: About, Candidates, Companies, Trust, Pricing, Contact

Interaction and layout guidance:
- Above the fold must be simple and decisive
- Do not over-design the hero
- Keep max content width controlled
- Use large spacing between sections
- Make mobile layout feel first-class, not compressed desktop
- Cards should be consistent in radius, shadow, spacing, and border treatment
- Keep CTA styling consistent across the page

What to emphasize visually:
- The interview-to-profile transformation
- Proof and trust
- Candidate momentum
- Clarity over cleverness

What to avoid:
- No generic AI robot art
- No stock photos of people in suits
- No busy dashboards on the homepage
- No too-many-CTAs problem
- No equal split hero for candidates and employers
- No dark, intimidating, enterprise-heavy feel
- No vague copy like “revolutionize hiring with AI”
- No overuse of glassmorphism or floating effects
- No purple/blue futuristic startup style

Design outcome:
The final landing page should make a first-time candidate immediately feel:
- I understand what this is
- This feels credible
- This seems better than applying with a resume
- I want to start the interview now
```
